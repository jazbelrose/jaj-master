The Print of 2023 : Retro-Futuristic-Renaissance

  • By J&J
  • May 8, 2021 - 6 min read

The dawn of 2023 sees a renaissance in textile design that takes us back in time while hinting at a more vibrant future. Retro is not just back; it’s making a bold and colorful statement.

Going beyond the classic patterns of the past, the year has seen a fusion of old-world charm with contemporary aesthetics. This blend is shaping up the fashion world in an enchanting way. The retro revival has led designers to explore the archives, drawing inspiration from the eclectic 60s and 70s, while adding a modern twist to cater to the tastes of the new generation.

In a delightful throwback, Dolce & Gabbana’s signature dolce vita aesthetic took a fresh turn with roses on dark backgrounds juxtaposed with swirling geometric prints. This sentiment was echoed by Marc Jacobs, where models graced the runway adorned in psychedelic floral dresses, accentuated with headscarves that screamed vintage chic.

Gucci, never one to be left behind in embracing the avant-garde, has redefined retro. Florals, reminiscent of decades gone by, were masterfully integrated into Alessandro Michele's signature eccentric, maximalist designs. The fusion of old and new has been the key, making pieces not just outfits but conversation starters.

As we venture deeper into 2023, embracing retro seems less about nostalgia and more about reinvention. The mantra is clear: go bold or go home. Whether it’s the vibrant hues, the oversized patterns, or the intriguing fabric choices, the textiles are meant to evoke emotion. It’s reminiscent of the times when floral patterns adorned living room walls, armchairs, and yes, even the ceilings. If a fabric reminds you of a bygone era, yet feels perfect for today's world, it's exactly what you should be wearing.

This year, textiles are not just about wearing; they are about experiencing. The retro wave is more than a trend, it's a movement. It urges us to reminisce, relive, and reinvent. And in this dance between the past and present, fashion finds its rhythm.
