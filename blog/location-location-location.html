Location Location Location

  • By J&J
  • October 5, 2022 - 7 min read

It’s not all about the venue; attendees are looking to the destination as a whole to provide unique offerings.

A destination must appeal and complement the event, particularly as a lot of time is spent in hotels, networking, or with scheduled free time. You don’t want the event to fall flat because attendees are holed up in a motel, miles away from activities when they break for the day. Choosing a destination that opens up further opportunities is important. We look at the meetings and events industry trends influencing event destinations and how they can prepare for event planners. Make a radical change in your lifestyle and begin to boldly do things which you may previously never have thought of doing, or been too hesitant to attempt. Once you become accustomed to such a life, you will see its full meaning and its incredible beauty.

Destination Playgrounds

Add fun and adventure to corporate events by turning the destination into a playground to enjoy and use. This involves play across the city, creating an atmosphere as well as activity planning. Seasonal events are an excellent example of how cities can unify through themes, and this becomes a creative starting point for event planners. Winter wonderlands, summer fayres, or local events can set the tone for the venue and extend the event theme into the city as if all were participants.

City Immersion

With more time spent on leisure than business during a work trip, traveling attendees need to be reached outside the venue. Destinations should offer immersive experiences to extend the event floor and message. Oversized art is one form through which this trend is coming to fruition—installing large custom pieces of artwork for photo opportunities is more exciting for guests than the standard photo booth. Larger destination cities provide more room for interactive oversized art, offering more opportunities to educate attendees via art and showcase the destination.

Some destinations are inevitably linked in our minds with certain concepts, often leading to preconceived notions. It requires the effort of many players, from local authorities to small business owners, to overcome this. Focus on the finer details of a destination, whether that’s experiences, wildlife, local cuisine, or culture, and capitalize on that by showing a new side of the destination that exceeds expectations. Destinations previously known to appeal to one demographic are starting to consider their offerings in other markets, surprising attendees and overcoming the odds stacked against them.

Authentic Connections

Authentic connections help make a destination resonate, allowing attendees to form bonds that lead them to discover all the location has to offer. Creating that connection could be as simple as bringing puppies into an internal meeting. The thought itself is exciting, and it transforms an otherwise standard business meeting into a memorable and positive experience. By partnering with a local animal shelter to bring the puppies in, the group forms a deeper connection with the destination.

Joining forces helps event destinations provide packages and bids that supersede the competition. While one party may inevitably end up the bigger winner, there are benefits to "co-opetition" and working together when targeting source markets or large events.
